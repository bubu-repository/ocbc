Competitive position

Followers and engagement rate only say how big and how quiet each account is. What they post, and why one post spikes, is the part worth copying or avoiding.

READ

Every competitor's biggest spike is either a sponsorship (UOB, HSBC) or an accident (CIMB Niaga's service-migration backlash mirrors OCBC's own comment problem in Section 06). None of the four has built an owned content property.

MECHANISM

Sponsorship content borrows an athlete's or partner's audience for a one-off spike, likes without conversation, and it disappears once the event ends. It never becomes a habit the account owns. A service-change post spiking comments is not engagement, it is customers venting because nothing else in the feed gives them a channel.

MOVE

Do not copy sponsorship-for-likes or chase CIMB Niaga's follower count. The two-way quadrant is empty because sponsorships end and generic lifestyle content never starts a conversation. OCBC's licensed IP, paired with the BCA-style owned property in Section 05, is the only lever in this set built to last.

UNIVERSITIES TAGGED IN THE SERIES

Genera-Z Battle is a BCA-owned dance and talent competition between university teams (UGM vs UNPAD, UIN Sunan Gunung Djati, Universitas Cenderawasih), posted as Reels and tagging each campus account directly. It is not licensed IP, BCA built it, and it runs 56x the account's typical post.

READ

BCA's baseline post performs worse than OCBC's (roughly 55 likes vs 255), but one owned property, a university battle series, produces the platform's biggest organic spikes, and a gamified loyalty feature (Gacha BCA) outperforms every giveaway on comments.

MECHANISM

Tagging a university turns every post into inter-campus pride. Students and alumni share to back their team, which is a free distribution channel no paid boost can buy. Gacha BCA rewards existing customers with a game mechanic tied to real spend, not a follow-and-tag contest, so it builds habit instead of renting a comment.

MOVE

OCBC rents Disney's fanbase. BCA built its own with students. OCBC's licensed IP has the same shareability lever available, pair it with a tribal or competitive mechanic (city vs city, campus vs campus) instead of a static card render, and replace the giveaway with a loyalty game tied to real product use.

08

BUBU playbook

BUBU POSITION

OCBC buys attention weekly and owns none of it. The licence and the serial are the only assets that earn attention unpaid. Build the system around them.

It's not about visibility. It's about relevance. OCBC has the visibility; the five moves below convert it.

1

Nyala Drops: a release program for the IP cards

Fixed reveal calendar, numbered limited editions, and a show-your-card UGC ritual. The card becomes the reason to follow the account instead of a promo backdrop.

DATA Toy Story 768 median likes, 3.0x baseline, with zero community mechanics attached today. BCA's owned Genera-Z Battle series proves the tribal mechanic works at 56x its baseline (05).
2

Scale the serial into a money-life universe

Three recurring characters across life stages: first salary, new parent, sandwich generation. Episodic content builds a returning audience; one-offs do not.

DATA Diari Bapak Baru 6,330 median views vs 4,150 generic Reel baseline, no paid support.
3

Reply Desk: answer the thread, publicly

Same-day replies with a human voice, PII stripped on sight, and a monthly format answering the three most repeated complaints. Service proof becomes content.

DATA 99 comments on the flagship post, 0 visible brand replies, 2 doxxing posts live.
4

Creator roster replaces the Friday giveaway

Move the giveaway budget to 8-10 finance and lifestyle micro-creators on always-on briefs. Creator credibility compounds; contest comments reset to zero every week.

DATA Giveaways bought 92.5% of comments. BCA's Gacha loyalty feature drew 316 comments with no tag-3-friends mechanic, real usage, not contest entries (05).
5

Fewer, better, timed

Hero IP Reels on Thu, one fraud PSA a week, carousels only when the format earns it. Redirect boost budget from corporate films to proven IP content.

DATA Thu median 311 vs Tue 209 (+49%). Carousels: 61% of output at baseline. Boosted film: 0.09% like rate.
