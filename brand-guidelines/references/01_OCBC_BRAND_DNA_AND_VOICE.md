## 1. Who OCBC Indonesia Is

OCBC Indonesia (formerly Bank OCBC NISP, unified under the global OCBC master brand on 14 November 2023) sells discipline, not luck. The bank's central idea is **Financial Fitness**: financial health behaves like physical health. It is built through repeated habits (budgeting, saving, investing, protecting), not a single windfall or a single smart trade. Every piece of content this brand publishes should trace back to that idea. If a caption could run under any bank's logo, it fails.

The global brand promise sits above every local campaign: **"For now, and beyond"**: OCBC positions itself as reliable, knowledgeable, and long-term in every dealing, with a stated purpose of "lifting others" (customers, small businesses, and the communities around them). Financial Fitness is the Indonesian market's local proof point for that global promise: it turns "long-term view" into a specific, teachable set of habits.

Two branded vehicles carry the Financial Fitness idea into content, and they are not interchangeable:

- **NYALA**: the individual-facing delivery brand. It is the product line ("Nyala: Tabungan & Investasi Fleksibel," a flexible savings-and-investment account), the community platform **Ruang meNYALA** (ruangmenyala.com) with its Financial Fitness Classes, Financial Fitness Squad mentoring, and Financial Fitness Gym content series, and the home of the annual **OCBC Financial Fitness Index (FFI)** research (2024 edition ran the theme "FUNanciallyFIT"). Audience: Gen Z entering their first salary years and Millennials building family wealth. Content here teaches budgeting frameworks, investment literacy (reksa dana, SBN, saham), insurance and protection planning, and retirement math. The register is coach, not lecturer.
- **Nyala Bisnis**: the entrepreneur/UMKM-facing sibling brand, part of OCBC's broader SME suite alongside OCBC Business (digital SME banking) and Ruang Kreasi (OCBC's UMKM creative and marketing hub). Nyala Bisnis's core proof point is a combined personal-and-business account that keeps two ledgers under one login, fee-free. Content here covers business cash flow, separating personal and business finance, working capital, and founder stories, including OCBC's women-entrepreneur program ("Berani Cuantik"). The register is peer-to-peer: a bank that has sat across the table from a warung owner and a scaling F&B franchise alike.

Positioning statement to hold in mind when drafting anything:

> OCBC Indonesia treats every customer as an athlete of their own finances, and gives them the coaching (NYALA / Nyala Bisnis), the tools (the account or product itself), and the community (Ruang meNYALA, Ruang Kreasi) to train.

Every closing line, CTA, or sign-off should be checkable against the "For now, and beyond" promise: does this piece of content help the reader now, and does it point at a benefit that compounds later? If it only does one of the two, it is an ad, not Financial Fitness content.

## 2. Tone of Voice Matrix

| Dimension | What We Sound Like | What We Never Sound Like |
|---|---|---|
| Authority | A senior advisor who explains the "why" before the "what" | A regulator reading out a disclosure |
| Energy | Confident, forward-leaning, calm under numbers | Hyped, exclamation-mark-heavy, salesy |
| Language | Bilingual by design: Indonesian for warmth and proximity, English for financial precision (e.g., "cash flow," "compound interest," "Financial Fitness Index") | Full corporate Bahasa baku that reads like a annual report footnote |
| Humor | Dry, situational, tied to money behavior ("gajian lupa nabung" is material; a meme format is not) | Slapstick, meme-account voice, self-deprecating to the point of undermining trust |
| Data | Cites real figures (OJK statistics, BPS data, OCBC's own Financial Fitness Index) with a source line | Vague claims like "banyak orang" or "riset menunjukkan" with no attribution |
| Address | Speaks to "kamu" in consumer content, "Anda" only in formal LinkedIn/B2B contexts | Switches register mid-post; addresses the reader as a case study instead of a person |
| Ambition | Growth-minded, treats wealth-building as a skill anyone can train | Preachy about frugality, or aspirational in a way that reads as out of reach (private-jet flexing) |

## 3. Copywriting Rules by Format

### 3.1 LinkedIn Thought Leadership

- Open with a claim or a number in the first line: LinkedIn truncates after ~140 characters, so the hook must survive the "see more" cut alone.
- Structure: **Claim → Evidence → Mechanism → Implication for the reader's decision.** Do not structure as "Did you know...?" questions; OCBC's LinkedIn voice states positions, it doesn't quiz the reader.
- One data point per paragraph, sourced (OJK, BPS, Bank Indonesia, or OCBC's internal Financial Fitness Index study).
- Close on a specific action or a sharpened restatement of the claim, never on "What do you think? Comment below!"
- Length: 900–1,300 characters for standalone text posts, 150–200 words for card carousels.
- Titles/headers on LinkedIn Cards assert a position: "SBN Ritel Bukan Investasi Bebas Risiko," not "Tentang SBN Ritel."

### 3.2 Instagram Educational Captions

- First line is the payoff, not the setup: Instagram cuts captions after roughly 125 characters before "more."
- Explain one concept per carousel. Do not stack three unrelated tips under one post; that is a listicle, not financial coaching.
- Use "kamu" throughout. Mix in English financial terms deliberately (they signal literacy and are searchable): "dana darurat" alongside "emergency fund," "reksa dana pasar uang" alongside "money market fund."
- Every educational post ends with one concrete next step ("Cek Financial Fitness Index kamu di ocbc.id/fitness"), not a generic "swipe up to learn more."
- Captions read like a colleague explaining something over coffee who happens to know the regulation cold, not like a textbook.

### 3.3 Reels/Short-Form Hooks

- First 1.5 seconds carries the entire hook: on-screen text plus spoken line must land before a thumb decides to scroll.
- Hook formats that work for this brand: a corrected misconception ("SBN Ritel itu BUKAN cuma buat yang udah tua"), a real number ("Rp 500 ribu per bulan bisa jadi Rp 87 juta dalam 10 tahun, ini itungannya"), a direct address to a decision the viewer is putting off ("Kamu udah punya dana darurat belum? Cek dulu sebelum lanjut nonton").
- No countdown hooks ("3 alasan kenapa...") as an opener; state the sharpest reason first, let the count reveal itself in the body.
- Scripts run 20–45 seconds. Every script ends on the sharpest line, not a recap of the three points just made.

## 4. Banned Language

Do not use these in any OCBC Indonesia content, in either English or Indonesian, regardless of format:

**English:** delve, tapestry, testament, crucial, paramount, pivotal, foster, robust, seamless, leverage (as a verb meaning "use"), utilize, synergy, actionable, game-changing, holistic, elevate, empower, unlock, harness, "navigating the financial landscape," "in today's fast-paced world," "it is worth noting."

**Indonesian equivalents to avoid for the same reason (generic, AI-flavored, says nothing a specific fact couldn't say better):** "di era digital saat ini," "sangat penting untuk diketahui," "menavigasi lanskap keuangan," "memberdayakan," "menciptakan sinergi," "solusi holistik," "langkah krusial."

**Structural bans:**
- No "It's not just X, it's Y" contrast frames.
- No reflexive triads ("smart, simple, and secure") unless all three words are independently load-bearing and specific to the claim.
- No "Let's dive in" or any variant as a transition.
- No recap endings ("Jadi, intinya...", "To summarize..."). End on the sharpest point, not a summary of points already made.
- Headers assert a position ("Reksa Dana Pasar Uang Bukan Tabungan Biasa"), they don't label a topic ("Tentang Reksa Dana").

## 5. Quick Voice Check

Before publishing, run the copy against these four questions:

1. Would this sentence survive if you deleted every adjective from the banned list? (If not, replace the adjective with a fact.)
2. Does the first line work with the rest of the post hidden behind "more"?
3. Is there one number, one source, and one concrete action in this piece?
4. Could a competitor's logo replace OCBC's without the copy reading strangely? If yes, it isn't specific enough to OCBC's Financial Fitness thesis. Rewrite.
